TO: HEAD — PRODUCTS & INVESTMENT STRATEGY

A journey through market history — and the Axis story within it

A proposal for a series of learning interventions for the Axis sales team

THE BEGINNING

You have been part of this journey from the very beginning — so you know this better than most.

Axis was born in 2009, in the immediate aftermath of the Global Financial Crisis. At a moment when global capital markets had just experienced their most violent dislocation in decades, and when investor confidence was at its most fragile, a group of people chose to build a fund house. That founding decision — to start something ambitious at the hardest possible moment — says something important about the character of Axis.

From that beginning, Axis became the fastest growing asset management company in India. The funds we built — anchored in a clear Quality philosophy — grew alongside a generation of investors who were discovering the power of equity investing. Our flagship funds — Axis LTE, Bluechip, and Focused — delivered exceptional long-term returns and built deep, trusting relationships with distributors and clients across the country.

In the years since, a lot has changed — in the Indian economy, in global markets, in the way investors think about their money, and in the breadth and depth of what Axis offers. We have had our crests and our troughs. We have navigated rate cycles, style rotations, credit events, valuation dislocations, and everything in between. Through it all, the Axis story has continued to be written.

What we are proposing today is an attempt to make sure our sales team knows that story — deeply, with pride, and with the ability to use it in every client conversation they have.

THE OPPORTUNITY

The Axis sales team knows the products well. What many of them lack — through no fault of their own — is the historical and economic context that makes those products meaningful in a conversation.

When a distributor asks why a Quality fund underperformed a PSU fund for three consecutive years, the answer that retains the relationship requires an understanding of rate cycles, PE compression mechanics, and why Value styles lead in certain macro environments. When a client asks whether to stay invested through a correction, the answer that builds long-term trust requires an understanding of how Indian markets have historically behaved.

Most of our sales team joined Axis during a period of extraordinary success for Quality investing — 2010 to 2021 was largely a golden era for the style our flagship funds represent. Many of them have never experienced, or never had to explain, a prolonged period where the market rewarded a very different kind of investing. 2022 and 2025 were those tests. Some of our team passed confidently. Others found themselves without the language to hold the conversation.

This is not a deficit to be ashamed of. It is a natural consequence of growing rapidly in a favourable environment. The remedy is equally natural: a structured, engaging, and genuinely enjoyable journey through the history of markets and Axis together.

Done well, this does not feel like training. It feels like rediscovering a story you are already part of.

THE PROPOSAL — A SERIES OF EPISODES

We are proposing the Market Judgment Capability Programme — a series of focused learning episodes, each one a chapter in the larger narrative of how Axis and Indian markets have grown together since 2009.

The programme is not a curriculum. It is closer to an exhibition — one that invites the sales team to understand the world their funds were born into, the economic cycles they have lived through, and the product innovations that reflected the thinking of a fund house that was always looking ahead. Each episode is anchored in real economic history, real Axis data, and the real situations the team encounters in the field.

Each episode stands alone but is richer in context of the others. The series is designed to be delivered over the course of a year — one episode at a time, allowing us to pilot, learn, and continuously improve. The programme grows stronger with each chapter added.

The ambition is simple: a sales team that carries the Axis story with genuine pride, that can hold any market conversation with confidence, and that sees every challenging distributor question not as a threat but as an opportunity to demonstrate the depth of their understanding.
